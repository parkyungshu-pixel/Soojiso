How it works

The IMEI (International Mobile Equipment Identity) is a unique 15-digit number assigned to every mobile device. The first 8 digits form the TAC (Type Allocation Code), and the first 2 digits identify the Reporting Body that allocated the device code, which maps to a specific country or region.
